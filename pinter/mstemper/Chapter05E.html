      Conclusion: A×B is not a cyclic group.

      Proof: Let gcd(m,n)=k>1. Then, let m=pk and n=qk.

      Let a∈A and b∈B. Then am=e and bn=e. (a,b)pqk = (apqk,bpqk) = ((apk)q,(bqk)p) = ((am)q,(bn)p) = (eq,ep) = (e,e). Therefore, (a,b) can generate at most pqk distinct elements of A×B. Since A×B has mn=pqkk>pqk elements, (a,b) does not generate A×B.

  8. If G is generated by two elements, a and b, and ab=ba, show that G is Abelian.

    First, a lemma: if pq=qp then pq-1=q-1p.

    pq-1 = epq-1 = q-1qpq-1 = q-1pqq-1 = q-1pe = q-1p.

    Another lemma: if ij=ji then any product of is and js may be re-ordered so that all of the is are followed by all of the js.

    Any product that consists exclusively of is or exclusively of js is already in this form, so we only need to consider products that contain at least one i and one j.

    Base For n=2, ji=ij.

    Hypothesis Assume that for some positive k, a product of k factors may be so re-ordered.

    Step Let z be a product of k factors that has been so ordered. Then, a product of k+1 factors is either zi or zj. In the case of zi, the last factor of z is a j. Commute this with the trailing i. Now, we have a product of k is and js followed by a j. This product may be re-ordered, as per the Induction Hypothesis. Doing so leaves us with all is followed by all js. In the case of zj, the product is already in that form.

    Finally, the main result. Let y be any product of any number of terms that are any of a, b, a-1, and b-1. By the first lemma, all of these elements commute with each other. Any sub-product containing multiple occurrences of any two of these may be re-ordered so that all of its as preceed all of its a-1s, which preceed all of its bs, which preceed all of its b-1s. This may be repeated on ever-larger sub-products until the entire product is order this way.

    Since all of the elements of G are composed of products of varying numbers of as, a-1s, bs, and b-1s, the product of two elements of G also is. For any two elements, p and q, we may re-order the factors of pq and the factors of qp in this manner, showing that they are equal. Therefore, G is Abelian.
