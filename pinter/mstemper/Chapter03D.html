Problem D — A Checkerboard Game

On a two-by-two square, define the following movements:

  • V — move vertically
  • H — move horizontally
  • D — move diagonally
  • I — stay put

Let G = {V, H, D, I}. Define X*Y as "make move X followed by move Y."

  1. Show the operation table for <G, *>.

    * I V H D
    I I V H D
    V V I D H
    H H D I V
    D D H V I

  2. Assuming associativity, show that this is a group.

    I (staying put) is obviously the identity.

    Each element is its own inverse.

    In all cases, X*Y = Y*X, so it is also an Abelian group.

    (If we define T = {M,S} as "move" and "stay" on a one-by-two board, we can then view G as the cross-product of T with itself. G = T×T. Then, since T is a group, G is a group.)
