Problem B — Subgroups of Functions

For each of the following, show that H is a subgroup of G.

  1. G = <F(R), +>, H = {f∈F(R): f(x)=0 ∀x∈[0,1]}.

    If p,q∈H, then ∀x∈[0,1], [p+q](x)=0, so H is closed under addition.

    If p∈H, then ∀x∈[0,1], -p(x)=0, so H is closed under negation.

    Since H is closed under addition and negation, H is a subgroup of G.

  2. G = <F(R), +>, H = {f∈F(R): f(-x)=-f(x)}.

    If p,q∈H, then [p+q](-x)=[p+q](-x), so H is closed under addition.

    If p∈H, then q(x)=p(-x)=-f(x) is, so H is closed under negation.

    Since H is closed under addition and negation, H is a subgroup of G.

  3. G = <F(R), +>, H = {f∈F(R): f is periodic with a period of π}.

    Note that "f is periodic with a period of π" means that ∀x f(x)=f(x+π).

    If p,q∈H, then [p+q](x)=[p+q](x+π), so H is closed under addition.

    If p∈H, then -f(x)=-f(x+π) is, so H is closed under negation.

    Since H is closed under addition and negation, H is a subgroup of G.

  4. G = <C(R), +>, H = {f∈C(R): ∫(0:1) f(x) dx = 0}.

    If p,q∈H, then ∫(0:1) p(x) dx = 0 and ∫(0:1) q(x) dx = 0. Since the integral of the sum of two functions equals the sum of their integrals, ∫(0:1) [p+q](x) dx = 0, and [p+q]∈H, and H is closed under addition.

    If p∈H, then ∫(0:1) p(x) dx = 0. Since the integral of a constant times a function is equal to the constant times the integral of the function, ∫(0:1) -p(x) dx = -1 * ∫(0:1) p(x) dx = -1 * 0 = 0, and H is closed under negation.

    Since H is closed under addition and negation, H is a subgroup of G.

  5. G = <D(R), +>, H = {f∈D(R): df/dx is a constant}.

    If p,q∈H, then dp/dx=a and dq/dx=b, where a and b are constants. Since the derivative of the sum of two functions is equal to the sum of their derivatives, d[p+q]/dx=a+b, which is a constant, and [p+q]∈H, and H is closed under addition.

    If p∈H, then dp/dx=a, where a is a constant. Since the derivative of a constant times a function is the constant times the derivative of the function, d[-p]/dx = d[-1*p]/dx = -1*(dp/dx) = -a, which is a constant and H is closed under negation.

    Since H is closed under addition and negation, H is a subgroup of G.

  6. G = <F(R), +>, H = {f∈F(R): f(x)∈Z ∀x∈R}.

    If p,q∈H, then for any y∈R, p(y)∈Z and q(y)∈Z. Therefore, [p+q](y) = p(y)+q(y)∈Z, since Z is closed under addition. Therefore, [p+q]∈H and H is closed under addition.

    If p∈H, then for any y∈R, p(y)∈Z. If p(y)∈Z. If p(y)∈Z, then -p(y)∈Z, since Z is closed under multiplication. Therefore, -p∈H and H is closed under negation.

    Since H is closed under addition and negation, H is a subgroup of G.
