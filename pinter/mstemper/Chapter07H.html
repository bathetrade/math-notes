Problem H — Properties of Permutations of a Set A

  1. Let A be a set, with a∈A. Let G = {f∈SA : f(a)=a}. Prove that G is a subgroup of SA.

    Let p,q∈G. Then, [p∘q](a) = p(q(a)) = p(a) = a, so p∘q∈G, and G is closed under composition.

    Let h∈G. Then, [h-1∘h](a) = h-1(h(a)) = h-1(a). However, for any x, [h-1∘h](x) = x, by the definition of inverse. Therefore h-1(a) = a, and h-1∈G, and G is closed under inversion.

    Since G is closed under composition and inversion, it is a subgroup of SA.

  2. If f is a permutation on A and a∈A, then f moves a if and only if f(a)≠a. Let G⊂SA where f∈G if and only if f moves a finite number of elements of A. Prove that G is a subgroup of SA.

    Let r,s∈G. Then, r moves a finite number of elements, m, and s moves a finite number of elements, n. Then, r∘s can not move any more than m+n elements. Since m and n are finite, m+n is, and r∘s∈G. Therefore, G is closed under composition.

    Let t∈G. Then, t moves a finite number of elements. Since t-1 must move the same elements back to their original positions, t-1 must also move a finite number of elements, and t-1∈G. Therefore, G is closed under inversion.

    Since G is closed under composition and inversion, it is a subgroup of SA.

  3. Let A be a finite set, and let B⊂A be non-empty. Let G = {f∈SA : x∈B ⇒ f(x)∈B}. Show that G is a subgroup of SA.

    Let f,g∈G. Select any x∈B. Then g(x)∈B, and so is f(g(x)). Therefore, [f∘g](x)∈B. This shows that G is closed under composition.

    Permutations are one-to-one, so for any h∈G, |h(B)| = |B|. Since A is finite, B is, and so is h(B). Therefore, the pre-image of any x∈B is also in B. This shows that G is closed under inversion.

    Since G is closed under composition and inversion, it is a subgroup of SA.

  4. Give an example to show that the conclusion of Exercise 3 does not apply if A is an infinite set.

    Let A = Z, and let B = {2i : i∈Z+∪{0}}. Define:

    f(x) x+2 x∈B
    x+1 x<0
    x otherwise

    This is a permutation on A, and x∈B⇒f(x)∈B. However, the pre-image of 0 under f is -1, which is not in B, so f-1∉G. Since G is not closed under inversion, it is not a group.
