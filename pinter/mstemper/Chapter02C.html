  2. Which of O0 through O15 are commutative?

    O0, O1, O6, O7, O8, O9, O14, and O15 are commutative operations. For each of the others, ab≠ba.

  3. Which of O0 through O15 are associative?

    O0, O1, O3, O5, O6, O7, O9, and O15 are associative operations. The others are not, because:

    O2(ba)bb(ab)
    O4(ba)bb(ab)
    O8(aa)ba(ab)
    O10(aa)ba(ab)
    O11(ab)aa(ba)
    O12(aa)ba(ab)
    O13(ab)aa(ba)
    O14(aa)ba(ab)

    Of these, O1, O6, O7, and O9 have a two-sided identity element.

  5. In which of O0 through O15 does each element have an inverse?

    We will only examine O1, O6, O7, and O9, since only they have two-sided identity elements.

    • In O1, there is not an inverse for a.
    • In O6, each element is its own inverse.
    • In O7, there is not an inverse for b.
    • In O9, each element is its own inverse.
