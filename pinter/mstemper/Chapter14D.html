  2. Prove that every subgroup of an Abelian group is normal.

    Let G be an Abelian group, with x ∈ G. Let H be a subgroup of G, with a ∈ H.

    Since G is Abelian, xax-1 = axx-1 = ae = a ∈ H. Since the conjugate of any element of H with any element of G is in H, H is normal with respect to G.

  3. Prove that the center of any group G is a normal subgroup of G.

    Let x ∈ G. Let C be the center of G, with a ∈ C.

    Since the elements of the center of a group commute with every member of the group, xax-1 = axx-1 = ae = a ∈ C. Since the conjugate of any element of C with any element of G is in C, C is normal with respect to G.

  4. With H a subgroup of G, prove that H is normal iff ∀ a,b ∈ G, ab ∈ H iff ba ∈ H.

    If Let x,y ∈ G. Then, xy ∈ G and x-1 ∈ G. Then, (xy)x-1 ∈ H iff x-1(xy) ∈ H; in other words, the conjugate of y ∈ H iff y ∈ H.

    Therefore, if ∀ a,b ∈ G, ab ∈ H iff ba ∈ H, then H is normal.

    Only If With H normal, let xy ∈ H. Then y(xy)y-1 ∈ H because H is normal. Since y(xy)y-1 = yx, yx ∈ H.

    Similarly, let yx ∈ H. Then x(yx)x-1 ∈ H because H is normal. This gives xy ∈ H.

    Therefore, H is normal leads to xy ∈ H iff yx ∈ H.

  5. Let H be a subgroup of G. Prove that H is normal iff aH = Ha ∀ a ∈ G.

    If If ∀ a ∈ G, aH = Ha, then aHa-1 = Haa-1 = H. Since aHa-1 is the set of all conjugates of elements of H, H is normal.

    Only If If H is normal, then axa-1 ∈ H ∀ x ∈ H. Then axa-1a = ax ∈ Ha. Since it is also true that ax ∈ aH, we have aH = Ha.

  6. Prove that any intersection of normal subgroups of G is a normal subgroup of G.

    Every subgroup of G contains e, so any intersection of subgroups of G contains it and is non-empty.

    If x,y are in two subgroups of G, then xy and yx are in those subgroups, and hence in their intersection. Therefore, any intersection of subgroups of G is closed under multiplication.

    If z is in two subgroups of G, then z-1 is in them, and hence in their intersection. Therefore, any intersection of subgroups of G is closed under inversion.

    If a is in two normal subgroups of G, then all of its conjugates must be in them, and hence in their intersection. Therefore, any intersection of normal subgroups of G is closed under conjugation.

    Since the intersection of any two normal subgroups of G is closed under multiplication, inversion, and conjugation, and is non-empty, it is a normal subgroup of G.
