Problem B — Elements Which Correspond Under an Isomorphism

Let G1 and G2 be groups, and let f:G1 → G2 be an isomorphism.

  1. If e1 denotes the neutral element of G1 and e2 denotes the neutral element of G2, prove that f(e1) = e2.

    Let a ∈ G1. Since f is an isomorphism, f(a) = f(e1a) = f(e1)f(a). Since f(a) = f(e1)f(a) and a group (such as G2) only has one identity element, f(e1) = e2.

  2. Prove that for each element a ∈ G1, f(a-1) = [f(a)]-1.

    As in the previous exercise, e1 is the identity element of G1 and e2 is the identity element of G2.

    aa-1 e1 inverses and identity
    f(a)f(a-1) f(e1) f is an isomorphism
    f(a)f(a-1) e2 previous exercise
    [f(a)]-1f(a)f(a-1) [f(a)]-1e2 pre-multiply by [f(a)]-1

  3. If G1 is a cyclic group with generator a, G2 is a cyclic group with generator f(a).

    The proof will be by induction on the powers of a.

    Base Since f is an isomorphism, f(a2) = f(aa) = f(a)f(a) = [f(a)]2.

    Hypothesis Assume that f(ak) = [f(a)]k.

    Step f(ak+1) = f(aka) = f(ak)f(a) = [f(a)]kf(a) = [f(a)]k+1.

    Therefore, all powers of a in G1 correspond to powers of f(a) in G2. Since f is a bijection, there are no other elements in G2.

    This means that G2 is a cyclic group, generated by f(a), and with the same order as G1.
