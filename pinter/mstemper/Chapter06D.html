Problem D — Composite Functions

  1. f:R→R, defined by f(x) = sin(x).
    g:R→R, defined by g(x) = ex.
    Find f∘g and g∘f.

    [f∘g](x) = sin(ex)
    [g∘f](x) = esin(x)

  2. f:A×B→B×A, defined by f(x,y) = (y,x).
    g:B×A→B, defined by g(y,x) = y.
    Find g∘f.

    [g∘f](x,y) = y.

  3. f:(0,1)→R, defined by f(x) = 1/x.
    g:R→R, defined by g(x) = ln(x).
    Find g∘f and explain why f∘g is undefined.

    [g∘f](x) = ln(1/x).

    f∘g is undefined because g is not defined on all of R, and because its image includes values not in the range of f.

  4. f:([a-z]*)→[a-z]*, defined by spelling each word backwards and exchanging all s's and t's.
    g:([a-z]*)→[a-z]*, defined by a's with o's, i's with u's, e's with y's, and s's with t's.
    Describe f∘g and g∘f. Are they the same?

    Both f∘g and g∘f replace "aeiouy" with "oyuaie", while reversing the order of letters in each word. "S" and "t" remain unchanged. Not only do f and g commute, but each is also its own inverse.

  5. Let A = {a, b, c, d} and let f:A→A and g:A→A be defined as:

    abcd
    acac

    abcd
    baba

    What is f∘g?

    abcd
    caca

    What is g∘f?

    abcd
    bbbb

  6. With G a group and a,b ∈G, let f:G→G be f(x)=ax and g:G→G be g(x)=bx, find f∘g and g∘f.

    [f∘g](x) = abx and [g∘f](x) = bax.

  7. Find the domain and range of each composite function from Exercises 1-6.

    Ex. f∘g g∘f
    Domain Range Domain Range
    1 R (-1,1) R (1/e,e)
    2 n/a n/a A×B B
    3 n/a n/a (0,1) (0,∞)
    4 [a-z]* [a-z]* [a-z]* [a-z]*
    5 {a, b, c, d} {a, c} {a, b, c, d} {b}
    6 G G G G
