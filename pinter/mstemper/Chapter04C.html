Problem C — Elements That Commute

Assuming that a∈G and b∈G commute (that is, ab=ba), prove each of the following:

  1. a-1 and b-1 commute

    a-1b-1 (ba)-1 property of inverses
    (ab)-1 a and b commute
    b-1a-1 property of inverses

  2. a and b-1 commute

    ab-1 ab-1e identity
    ab-1a-1a inverse
    aa-1b-1a a-1 and b-1 commute
    eb-1a inverse
    b-1a identity

  3. a commutes with ab

    a(ab) a(ba) a commutes with b
    (ab)a associativity

  4. a2 commutes with b2

    a2b2 (aa)(bb) definition of x2
    a(ab)b associativity
    (ab)ab a commutes with ab
    (ba)ab a commutes with b
    ba(ab) associativity
    b(ab)a a commutes with ab
    b(ba)a a commutes with b
    (bb)(aa) associativity
    b2a2 definition of x2

  5. ∀ x∈G, xax-1 commutes with xbx-1

    xax-1xbx-1 xaebx-1 inverse
    xabx-1 identity
    xbax-1 a commutes with b
    xbeax-1 identity
    xbx-1xax-1 inverse

  6. ab = ba iff aba-1 = b

    If:

    ab ba given
    aba-1 baa-1 post-multiply by a-1
    aba-1 be inverse
    aba-1 b identity

    Only if:

    aba-1 b given
    aba-1a ba post-multiply by a
    abe ba inverse
    ab ba identity

  7. ab = ba iff aba-1b-1 = e

    If:

    ab ba given
    aba-1 baa-1 post-multiply by a-1
    aba-1 be inverse
    aba-1 b identity
    aba-1b-1 bb-1 post-multiply by b-1
    aba-1b-1 e identity

    Only if:

    aba-1b-1 e given
    aba-1b-1b eb post-multiply by b
    aba-1e eb inverse
    aba-1 b identity
    aba-1a ba post-multiply by a
    abe ba inverse
    ab ba identity
