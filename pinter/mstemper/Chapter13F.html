Problem F — Survey of all Six-Element Groups

Let G be any group of order six. By Cauchy's Theorem, it has elements a with order 2 and b with order 3.

  1. Prove that ba ∉ {e, a, b, b2}.

    ba ≠ e If ba=e, then a = b-1. However, the order of an element is the same as the order of its inverse. Since ord(a) = 2 and ord(b) = 3, this cannot be true.

    ba ≠ a If ba=a, then b=e. Since ord(b) = 3 and ord(e) = 1, this cannot be true.

    ba ≠ b If ba=b, then a=e. Since ord(a) = 2 and ord(e) = 1, this cannot be true.

    ba ≠ b2 If ba=b2, then a=b. Since ord(a) = 2 and ord(b) = 3, this cannot be true.

  2. Prove that if ba=ab, then G ≘ Z6.

    One possible isomorphism is:

    Z6 G
    0 e
    1 ab, ba
    2 b2
    3 a
    4 b
    5 ab2

  3. Prove that if ba=ab2, then G ≘ S3.

    One possible isomorphism is:

    S3 G
    () e
    (12) a
    (23) ab
    (13) ab2, ba
    (123) b
    (321) b2
