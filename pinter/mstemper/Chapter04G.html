Problem G — Direct Products of Groups

The direct product of two groups G and H is defined as G×H = {(x,y): x∈G and y∈H}. If x, x' ∈ G and y, y' ∈ H, then (x,y)(x',y') = (xx',yy').

  1. Show that G×H is a group.

    Previously done elsewhere.

  3. Show that if G and H are Abelian groups, then G×H is Abelian.

    Let a,b ∈ G and c,d ∈ H.

    (a,c)+(b,d) (a+b,c+d) definition of addition in G×H
    (b+a,c+d) G is Abelian
    (b+a,d+c) H is Abelian
    (b,d)+(a,c) definition of addition in G×H

  4. Show that if every element of G and H is its own inverse, then every element of G×H is its own inverse.

    Let a ∈ G and c ∈ H.

    (a,c)+(a,c) (a+a,c+c) definition of addition in G×H
    (e,c+c) a∈G is its own inverse
    (e,e) c∈H is its own inverse
