Problem F — Orders of Powers of Elements

Let a be an element of order 12 in a group G.

  1. What is the smallest positive integer k such that a8k = e?

    By Theorem 5, a8k = e iff 8k = ord(a)q. Since ord(a) = 12, we have 8k = 12q. Since lcm(8,12) = 24, the smallest possible k is 24/8 = 3.

  2. What is the order of a8?

    Since a8k = (a8)k and k = 3 is the smallest positive integer giving a8k = e, it is also the smallest positive integer giving (a8)k = e. As this is the definition of "order of an element", ord(a8) = 3.

  3. What are the orders of a9, a10, a5?

    lcm(9,12) = 36, so ord(a9) = 36/9 = 4.

    lcm(10,12) = 60, so ord(a10) = 60/10 = 6.

    lcm(5,12) = 60, so ord(a5) = 60/5 = 12.

  4. Which powers of a have the same order as a?

    The powers that are relatively prime to ord(a). In this case, they are: 5, 7, and 11.

  5. Let a be an element of order m in a group G. What is the order of ak?

    ord(ak) = lcm(k,m)/k.

  6. Let a be an element of order m in a group G. For what values of k is ord(ak) = m?

    ord(ak) = m whenever k and m are relatively prime.
