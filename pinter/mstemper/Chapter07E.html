Problem E — A Subgroup of SR

For any real numbers a,b (a≠0), define fa,b(x) = ax+b.

  1. Prove that fa,b is a permutation of R.

    Let fa,b(p) = fa,b(q). Then, ap+b=aq+b, ap=aq, and p=q. (We can divide both sides by a because it is non-zero). Therefore, fa,b is one-to-one.

    For any z∈R, fa,b((z-b)/a) = z. Therefore, fa,b is onto R.

    Since fa,b is one-to-on and onto R, it is a permutation of R.

  2. Prove that fa,b∘fc,d = fac,ad+b.

    [fa,b∘fc,d](x) = fa,b(fc,d(x)) = fa,b(cx+d) = acx + (ad+b) = fac,ad+b(x).

  3. Prove that f-1a,b = f1/a,-b/a.

    First, we must find the identity. For all x∈R, f1,0(x)=x, so f1,0 is the identity element.

    By Exercise 2 of this problem, fa,b∘f1/a,-b/a = f(a(1/a),(a(-b/a)+b) = f1,0, which is the identity. Therfore, f-1a,b = f1/a,-b/a.

  4. Let G = {fa,b : a∈R-{0}, b∈R}. Prove that G is a subgroup of SR.

    1. By Exercise 1, G ⊂ SR.
    2. By Exercise 2, G is closed under composition.
    3. By Exercise 3, G is closed under inversion.
