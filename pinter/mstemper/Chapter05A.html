Problem A — Recognizing Subgroups

For each of the following, determine whether or not H is a subgroup of G.

  1. G = <R,+>, H = {log(a): a∈Q, a≥0}.

    Let x=log(a) and y= log(b), where a,b∈Q+. Then, x,y∈H. x+y = log(a)+log(b) = log(ab). Since ab∈Q+ when a and b are, (x+y)∈H and H is closed under addition.

    Let x=log(a), where a∈Q+. Then -x=log(1/a). Since (1/a)∈Q+ when a is, -x∈H and H is closed under negation.

    Since H is closed under addition and negation, it is a subgroup of G.

  2. G = <R,+>, H = {log(n): n∈Z, n≥0}.

    Let x=log(n), where n∈Z+. Then -x=log(1/n). Since (1/n)∉Z+, H is not closed under negation and hence not a subgroup of G (or a group at all).

  3. G = <R,+>, H = {x∈R: tan(x)∈Q}.

    Let x=π/4. Since tan(π/4)=1∈Q, x∈H. x+x=π/2 and tan(π/2) is undefined, and therefore not in Q. So H is not closed under addition and not a group.

  4. G = <R*,×>, H = {2m3n: m,n∈Z}.

    Let p=2i3j and q=2k3l with {i,j,k,l}⊂Z. Then pq = 2i+k3j+l. Since Z is closed under addition, 2i+k3j+l∈H and H is closed under multiplication.

    Let p=2i3j (i,j∈Z). Then (1/p)=2-i3-j. Since Z is closed under negation, H is closed under inversion.

    Since H is closed under multiplication and inversion, it is a subgroup of G.

  5. G = <R×R,+>, H = {(x,y)∈R×R: y=2x}.

    An equivalent defintion of H is {(x,2x): x∈R}. With p,q∈R, (p,2p)∈H and (q,2q)∈H. (p,2p)+(q,2q) = (p+q,2(p+q)). Since R is closed under addition and multiplication, (p+q,2(p+q))∈H, and H is closed under addition.

    The additive inverse of (p,2p) is (-p,-2p) = (-p,2(-p))∈H, so H is closed under negation.

    Since H is closed under addition and negation, it is a subgroup of G.

  6. G = <R×R,+>, H = {(x,y): x2+y2>0}.

    Let a=-b and c=-d. If either a or c is non-zero, then a2+c2>0 and b2+d2>0, so (a,c) and (b,d) are both in H. (a,c)+(b,d)=(0,0)∉H, and H is not closed under addition and therefore not a group.

  7. With C⊂D, show that P(C) is a subgroup of P(D) with the group operation from Chapter 3, Problem C.

    Let A∈P(C) and B∈P(C). Since (A+B)⊂B⊂C, (A+B)∈P(C). Therefore, P(C) is closed under addition.

    Since each set is its own inverse with respect to this operation, P(C) is closed under negation.

    Since P(C) is closed under addition and negation, it is a subgroup of P(D).
