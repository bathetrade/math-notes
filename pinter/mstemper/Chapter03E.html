Problem E — A Coin Game

With two coins at positions "A" and "B", define the following movements:

  • I — do nothing
  • M1 — flip over the coin at "A"
  • M2 — flip over the coin at "B"
  • M3 — flip over both coins
  • M4 — switch the coins
  • M5 — flip over the coin at "A"; then switch
  • M6 — flip over the coin at "B"; then switch
  • M7 — flip over both coins; then switch

Let G = {I, M1, M2, M3, M4, M5, M6, M7}. Define X*Y as "make move X followed by move Y."

  1. Show the operation table for <G, *>.

    * I M1 M2 M3 M4 M5 M6 M7
    I I M1 M2 M3 M4 M5 M6 M7
    M1 M1 I M3 M2 M5 M4 M7 M6
    M2 M2 M3 I M1 M6 M7 M4 M5
    M3 M3 M2 M1 I M7 M6 M5 M4
    M4 M4 M6 M5 M7 I M2 M1 M3
    M5 M5 M7 M4 M6 M1 M3 I M2
    M6 M6 M4 M7 M5 M2 I M3 M1
    M7 M7 M5 M6 M4 M3 M1 M2 I

  2. Assuming associativity, show that this is a group.

    I (staying put) is obviously the identity.

    M5 and M6 are inverses for each other. Every other element is its own inverse.

    Since we get to assume associativity, this is a group.

  3. Is this commutative?

    No. For example, M5*M4 ≠ M4*M5.
