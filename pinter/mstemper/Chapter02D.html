Problem D — Automata: The Algebra of Input/Output Sequences

  1. Prove that concatenation of strings is an associative operation.

    Let a, b, and c be three strings in an alphabet A. Let a = a1...ai, b = b1...bj, and c = c1...ck. Then (ab)c = (a1...aib1...bj)c1...ck = a1...aib1...bjc1...ck. Also a(bc) = a1...ai(b1...bjc1...ck) = a1...aib1...bjc1...ck. So, (ab)c = a(bc), and this is an associative operation.

  2. Show that concatenation of strings is not a commutative operation.

    Let a="ST" and let b="OP". Then, ab = "STOP" while ba = "OPST".

  3. Find the identity element for concatenation of strings.

    Let e = "" (i.e., the empty string). Then for any string, a, ea = a = ae.
