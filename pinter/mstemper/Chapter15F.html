Problem F — Quotient of a Group By its Center

Assume that the quotient G/C (where C is the center of G) is cyclic, generated by Ca.

  1. Prove that ∀x ∈ G, ∃ m such that Cx = Cam.

    Since Ca generates G/C, ∀ x ∈ G, ∃ m such that (Ca)m = Cx.

    Since C is the center of G, every element in it commutes with a. Therefore, (Ca)m = Cmam. As C is a subgroup, it is closed under multiplication, so Cm = C, giving (Ca)m = Cam = Cx. ∎

  2. Prove that ∀ x ∈ G, ∃ m such that x = cam, where c ∈ C.

    By Exercise 1, ∃ m such that Cx = Cam. Since e ∈ C, ex = x ∈ Cx = Cam. Therefore, ∃ c such that x = cam.

  3. ∀ x,y ∈ G, xy = yx.

    By Exercise 2, x may be written as c'am and y may be written as c''an. Thus, xy = c'amc''an = c'c''aman = c'c''am+n = c'c''an+m = c''c'anam = c''anc'am = yx.

  4. Conclude that if G/C is cyclic, G is Abelian.

    Assuming that G/C is generated by Ca leads to the conclusion that xy = yx — with no constraints on x or y — so the conclusion is inescapable, if unexpected.
