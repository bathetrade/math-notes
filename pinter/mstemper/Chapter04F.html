Problem F — Constructing Small Groups

In each of the following, let G be any group, and e its identity element.

  1. With a, b ∈G, prove:

    1. a2=a → a=e

      a2 a
      aa a definition of a2
      aaa-1 aa-1 post-multiply by a-1
      ae e inverse
      a e identity

    2. ab=a → b=e

      ab a
      a-1ab a-1a pre-multiply by a-1
      eb e inverse
      b e identity

    3. ab=b → a=e

      ab b
      abb-1 bb-1 post-multiply by b-1
      ae e inverse
      a e identity

  2. Show that any row of a group's Cayley table must contain each element exactly once.

    By Part 6 of Problem B, each element must appear in each row. Since there are only as many columns as there are elements, no element can appear twice in one row.

  3. Complete the Cayley table for a three-element group.

      e a b
    e e a b
    a a b e
    b b e a

  4. Complete the Cayley table for a four-element group in which each element is its own inverse.

      e a b c
    e e a b c
    a a e c b
    b b c e a
    c c b a e

  5. Complete the Cayley table for a four-element group in which some element other than the identity is its own inverse and some element is not its own inverse.

    I choose to let a be its own inverse. Then, since elements that aren't their own inverses come in pairs, b and c must be inverses of each other.

      e a b c
    e e a b c
    a a e c b
    b b c a e
    c c b e a

  6. Using part 3 of Problem E, show that the two previous groups are the only possible four-element groups.

    In both cases (all elements are their own inverses, and two elements are their own inverses), there is only one choice for placing each element in the Cayley table after dealing with multiplication by the identity element and inverses.

    By part 3 of Problem E, it is not possible to have three elements that are their own inverses, and the identity element is always its own inverse.
