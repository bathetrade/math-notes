Problem A — Examples of Finite Quotient Groups

In each of the following, G is a group and H ⊲ G. List the elements of G/H and then write the Cayley table of G/H.

  1. G = Z10, H = {0, 5}.

    G/H = {H, H+1, H+2, H+3, H+5}

    Its Cayley table is:

    + H H+1 H+2 H+3 H+4
    H H H+1 H+2 H+3 H+4
    H+1 H+1 H+2 H+3 H+4 H
    H+2 H+2 H+3 H+4 H H+1
    H+3 H+3 H+4 H H+1 H+2
    H+4 H+4 H H+1 H+2 H+3

    Explain why G/H ≘ Z/5Z.

    Because |H| = 2 and 10/2 = 5. Since Z/10Z only has one subgroup of order 5, any quotient group of Z/10Z with order 5 must be isomorphic to that subgroup.

  2. G = S3 and H = {ε, β, δ}.

    G/H = {H, H+α}.

    Its Cayley table is:

    + H H+α
    H H H+α
    H+α H+α H

  3. G = D4, H = {R0, R2}.

    G/H = {H, H∘R1, H∘R5, H∘R6}

    ∘ H H∘R1 H∘R5 H∘R6
    H H H∘R1 H∘R5 H∘R6
    H∘R1 H∘R1 H H∘R6 H∘R5
    H∘R5 H∘R5 H∘R6 H H∘R1
    H∘R6 H∘R6 H∘R5 H∘R1 H

  4. G = D4, H = {R0, R2, R4, R5}.

    G/H = {H, H∘R1}.

    ∘ H H∘R1
    H H H∘R1
    H∘R1 H∘R1 H

  5. G = Z4×Z2, H = ⟨(0,1)⟩.

    G/H = {H, H+(1,0), H+(2,0), H+(3,0)}.

    + H H+(1,0) H+(2,0) H+(3,0)
    H H H+(1,0) H+(2,0) H+(3,0)
    H+(1,0) H+(1,0) H+(2,0) H+(3,0) H
    H+(2,0) H+(2,0) H+(3,0) H H+(1,0)
    H+(3,0) H+(3,0) H H+(1,0) H+(2,0)

  6. G = P3, H = {∅, {1}}.

    G/H = {H, H+{2}, H+{3}, H+{2,3},

    + H H+{2} H+{3} H+{2,3}
    H H H+{2} H+{3} H+{2,3}
    H+{2} H+{2} H H+{2,3} H+{3}
    H{3} H+{3} H+{2,3} H H+{2}
    H+{2,3} H+{2,3} H+{3} H+{2} H
