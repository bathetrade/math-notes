    1. m=0

      a0 = e, so we have: aman = a0an = ean = an = a0+n = am+n.

    2. m<0 and n>0

      I'm less than pleased with my proof of this.

      If m<0, let -p = m (thus p>0), giving aman = a-pan. By definition, a-p = (a-1)p. This gives aman = (a-1)pan. We now need to look at three cases:

      • p<n In this case, p instances of a-1 cancel p instances of a, and we are left with an-p, or an+m.

      • p=n In this case, p instances of a-1 cancel p=n instances of a, and we are left with a0 = an+m.

      • p>n In this case, n instances of a-1 cancel n instances of a, and we are left with (a-1)p-n = (a-1)-m-n = (a-1)-(m+n) = am+n.
