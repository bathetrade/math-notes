Problem C — Isomorphism of Some Finite Groups

In each of the following, G and H are finite groups. Determine whether or not G ≅ H and prove it.

  1. G is the checkerboard game from Chapter 3, Problem D. H is the set {i, -i, 1, -1} under multiplication.

    In G, each element is its own inverse. In H, neither i nor -i is its own inverse, so G ≇ H.

  2. G is the same as in Exercise 1. H = Z4

    In G, each element is its own inverse. In H, neither 1 nor 3 is its own inverse, so G ≇ H.

  3. G is the group P2 of subsets of a two-element set with symmetric difference as the operation, as shown in Chapter 3, Problem C. H is {i, -i, 1, -1}, as in Exercise 1.

    As before, each element of G is its own inverse, while H has two elements that are not their own inverses, so G ≇ H.

  4. G is S3 and H is the group of matrices shown on pages 28 and 29 of the text.

    These groups are isomorphic. Element-by-element comparison of the products shows that the following function is an isomorphism from G to H:

    G H
    ε I
    (12) A
    (123) B
    (23) C
    (132) D
    (13) K

  5. G is the coin game of Chapter 3, Problem E. H is D4, the symmetries of the square, first shown in Chapter 5, Problem F, Exercise 2.

    Each of these groups have eight elements, exactly two of which are their own inverses. In each of these groups, the squares of each of the two elements that's not its own inverse is the same. In G, M5*M5 = M3 = M7*M7. In H, b*b = b2 = b3*b3.

    This is encouraging. Let's make the assumption that there is an isomorphism: either M5→b and M7→b3 or M7→b and M5→b3.

    In H, b*b3 = b3*b. If one of the proposed isomorphisms holds, then we should have M5*M7 = M7*M5 in G.

    However, M5*M7 = M2, while M7*M5 = M1. Therefore, these groups are not isomorphic.

  6. G is the group of symmetries of the rectangle (as in Chapter 7, Problem F, Exercise 2). H is {i, -i, 1, -1}, as in Exercise 1 of this problem.

    Once again, all of the elements of G are their own inverses. As in Exercise 1, H has two elements, i and -i, that are not their own inverses.

    G and H are not isomorphic.
